## What is Ketamine Crystal?
Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic originally developed in the 1960s for medical and veterinary use. In its crystal form, it's a white, crystalline powder that can be dissolved for various applications. Medically, ketamine is used for anesthesia, pain management, and increasingly for treating depression through FDA-approved therapies like esketamine nasal spray.

However, ketamine is a Schedule III controlled substance in the US and similarly regulated in many countries. Non-medical use, including recreational or unauthorized online purchases, is illegal and dangerous. Crystal ketamine is often associated with illicit markets, but legitimate access requires a prescription from a licensed provider.

### Medical vs. Illicit Use
- **Medical Benefits**: Ketamine shows promise in rapid antidepressant effects and chronic pain relief. Clinics offer supervised infusions for conditions like treatment-resistant depression.
- **Risks of Illicit Use**: Street ketamine can be impure, leading to overdose, bladder damage (ketamine cystitis), hallucinations, and addiction. Online sourcing amplifies risks due to scams, contaminated products, and legal repercussions.
